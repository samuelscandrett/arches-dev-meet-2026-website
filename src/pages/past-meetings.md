## International in-person meetings

Since 2024, the Arches developer and technical community have met up for two (soon to be three) in-person meetings. The purpose of every developer meetup is to allow attendees to share work with the community, network and build strong relationships with members of the community, learn about upcoming features and plans for the Arches platform, and identify areas of technical collaboration and roadmap development.

### Los Angeles, 2024

In March 2024, Arches software developers met at the Getty Center in Los Angeles for the first in-person, international Arches Developer Meeting, hosted by the [Getty Conservation Institute](https://www.getty.edu/conservation-institute/) and organised by [Farallon Geographics](https://www.fargeo.com/).

The 3-day meeting was attended by 30 developers, representing 11 organisations from across the world. The majority of the meeting consisted of community presentations, covering a wide range of topics such as Arches applications, accessibility, Arches version 8 (including Vue and changes to Graphs), a workflow builder, a plugin for QGIS, and many more. Many in-depth technical discussions also took place, in addition to a short collaborative coding sprint. 

For more information, including the complete list of presentation topics and outcomes, read the full report on the 2024 Arches Developer meeting on the [Arches Project website](https://www.archesproject.org/report-on-the-2024-arches-developer-meeting-in-los-angeles/).

### Belfast, 2025

In April 2025, developers and technically minded individuals connected once again for the second in-person developer meetup, this time hosted at Belfast Castle in Northern Ireland, hosted and organised by [Flax & Teal](https://flaxandteal.co.uk/), with support from the [Getty Conservation Institute](https://www.getty.edu/conservation-institute/).

The meeting was attended by great number of individuals from over a dozen organisations across the world. The 3 days consisted of action packed agenda, including presentations on the development and migration tools in Arches Lingo and the Controlled List Manager, Arches Application development with Arches QuerySets, reporting and dashboarding within Arches, utilising an LLM with Arches search, Modelling and data importing, and many more.  In addition to presentations, the final day of the meetup was structured as an unconference, allowing attendees to lead and engage in discussions on various topics.

For more information, including the complete list of presentation topics and outcomes, read the full report on the 2025 Arches Developer meeting on the [Arches Project website](https://www.archesproject.org/report-on-the-2025-arches-developer-meeting-in-belfast/).

## Online developer meetings

The Arches developer community have held a number of online developer meetings in the past. 

The most recent online developer meeting took place in October 2024 and was organised and hosted by [Galen Mancino](https://community.archesproject.org/u/whatisgalen) of [Scholium Technologies](http://scholiumtech.com/). The hour was structured of presentations, running in parallel, on the Arches ORM (Object Relational Mapping), data import pipelines, resource instance  and customising search in Arches. Each presentation was followed by a Q&A and wider discussion session.

The [full video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hDH8XCJZu3Y) can be found on the Arches Project YouTube channel.
